# Logitech MX Keyboard Battery / Backlight Notes

Session context: user asked whether low battery on a Logitech MX keyboard is a common problem or normal behavior.

## Authoritative baseline

Logitech product/support pages indicate that MX keyboard battery life depends heavily on backlighting:

- **MX Keys / MX Keys S**: advertised around **up to 10 days on a full charge with backlighting**, or **up to 5 months with backlighting turned off**.
- **MX Mechanical**: advertised around **up to 15 days on a full charge**, or **up to 10 months with backlighting turned off**.
- Logitech support for MX Keys says the keyboard shows low battery at **10% or below**; from **100% to 11%** the LED is green, and **10% or below** the LED is red.
- Logitech support also says the keyboard can continue typing for **500+ hours without backlighting when battery is low**, and can be used while charging over USB-C.

## Feature interactions

- MX keyboards use smart/adaptive backlighting and ambient/proximity behavior.
- Backlight may activate more often in dim rooms or when objects/hands/pets/vibration trigger proximity sensing.
- Support docs note backlighting may be disabled automatically in bright rooms or when battery is low.

## Practical interpretation

- Charging every **1–2 weeks** can be normal for MX keyboards if backlighting is enabled.
- If battery life becomes **months/weeks with backlighting off**, the keyboard is likely behaving as designed.
- If it drains in **a few days or less with backlighting off**, suspect battery aging, firmware/app issue, Bluetooth instability, or a defect.

## Recommended diagnostic

Run one complete charge cycle with **backlighting turned off**. If battery life dramatically improves, explain that the behavior is feature-driven rather than a defective battery. If it still drains quickly, suggest firmware/app update, testing another connection mode/host, then support/RMA if persistent.
