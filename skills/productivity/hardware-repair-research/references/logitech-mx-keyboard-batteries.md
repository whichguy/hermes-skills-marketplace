# Logitech MX keyboard battery replacement notes

Session context: user had a Logitech MX keyboard that worked for years and then suddenly could not keep a charge. The model number was later confirmed as **YR0073**.

## Diagnostic lesson

A Logitech MX keyboard needing charging every 1–2 weeks can be normal when backlighting is enabled, but a device that worked normally for years and then suddenly cannot keep a charge is more consistent with **lithium battery aging/failure**.

Useful distinction:

- Normal/backlight drain: runtime roughly matches manufacturer claims when backlight is enabled.
- Battery degradation: full charge drops quickly, low-battery warning returns soon, works while plugged in, drains even with backlight off, or loses charge while powered off.

## Official/spec anchors found

For full-size MX Keys / MX Keys S style keyboards:

- Battery life claim: up to **10 days with backlighting**, up to **5 months with backlighting off**.
- Low-battery LED threshold: red at **10% and below**.
- Logitech support notes the keyboard can keep typing for **500+ hours without backlighting** after low-battery warning.
- Smart backlighting / proximity lighting can materially affect runtime.

For MX Mechanical:

- Battery life claim: up to **15 days with backlighting**, up to **10 months with backlighting off**.

## YR0073 compatibility target

For Logitech **YR0073** full-size MX Keys / MX Keys S style replacement battery, look for:

- Model: **YR0073**
- Battery part: **533-000177**
- Nominal voltage: **3.7V**
- Capacity: **1500mAh**
- Energy: **5.55Wh**
- Approximate size seen in listings: **85.1 × 19.2 × 7.2mm**

Do **not** substitute MX Keys Mini or MX Mechanical batteries without confirming part number and dimensions.
